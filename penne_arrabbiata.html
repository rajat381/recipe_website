Preparation time: 25 Min
Difficulty: MEDIUM
Serves: 4-5 people
Category: Main Course
Posted by: Rajat
Calories 380
Protein 12g
Carbs 70g
Fat 8g
Saturates 1g
Fibre 5g
Sugar 8g
Salt 0.6g

Cooking Steps

1.
Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil and cook penne until al dente. Meanwhile, heat 4 tablespoons of good quality extra-virgin olive oil in a wide saucepan over medium heat. Add 4–6 crushed garlic cloves and cook until just golden, about 2 minutes.
2.
Add 1–2 teaspoons of dried red chilli flakes (or fresh peperoncino) to the olive oil — this is what makes it arrabbiata, meaning "angry" in Italian. Let the chilli sizzle for 30 seconds, then pour in 400g of crushed San Marzano tomatoes. Season with salt and stir well.
3.
Simmer the tomato sauce uncovered over medium heat for 15–18 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and the oil separates slightly on the surface. Taste and adjust chilli heat and salt. Add a pinch of sugar if the tomatoes are overly acidic.
4.
Drain the penne and add it directly to the sauce, reserving a splash of pasta water. Toss vigorously over medium heat for 1–2 minutes so the pasta absorbs the spicy tomato sauce. Add a splash of pasta water if needed to loosen and gloss up the dish.
5.
Remove from heat. Tear a generous handful of fresh flat-leaf parsley over the pasta and toss once more. Serve in bowls with a drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil. Traditional arrabbiata does not use cheese — the bold tomato and chilli are the star.
