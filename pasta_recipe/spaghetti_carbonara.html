Preparation time: 30 Min
Difficulty: HARD
Serves: 4-5 people

Step-by-Step Instructions

1.
Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente according to package directions. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining — this starchy water is the secret to a silky carbonara sauce.
2.
While pasta cooks, fry guanciale or pancetta in a large skillet over medium heat until golden and crispy, about 5–7 minutes. Remove the pan from heat. Whisk together egg yolks, whole egg, and finely grated Pecorino Romano in a bowl until smooth.
3.
Add hot drained spaghetti directly to the skillet with the guanciale (off the heat). Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, tossing constantly and adding reserved pasta water a little at a time to create a glossy, creamy sauce without scrambling the eggs.
4.
Season generously with freshly cracked black pepper — this is essential to carbonara, not optional. Taste and add a pinch of salt if needed. The sauce should coat the spaghetti in a velvety, pale-golden layer with visible bits of crispy guanciale throughout.
5.
Serve immediately in warmed bowls. Finish with extra grated Pecorino Romano and another crack of black pepper. Carbonara waits for no one — eat it right away while the sauce is still silky and the pasta perfectly hot.
