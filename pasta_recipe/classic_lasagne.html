Preparation time: 90 Min
Difficulty: HARD
Serves: 4-5 people
Category: Main Course
Posted by: Udyanshu Jakhar
Calories 620
Protein 35g
Carbs 52g
Fat 28g
Saturates 13g
Fibre 4g
Sugar 9g
Salt 1.1g

Preparation Steps

1.
Prepare the Bolognese ragù: Brown 500g minced beef and pork in a large saucepan with diced onion, carrot, and celery. Add a glass of red wine, let it evaporate, then stir in 400g crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and a bay leaf. Simmer on very low heat for at least 1 hour.
2.
Make the béchamel: Melt 60g butter in a saucepan, whisk in 60g plain flour, then gradually add 700ml warm whole milk, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Cook until thickened to a creamy consistency. Season with salt, white pepper, and freshly grated nutmeg.
3.
Pre-cook your lasagne sheets in boiling salted water for 2 minutes, then lay on oiled baking parchment. Preheat oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4. Lightly grease a deep baking dish and spread a spoonful of ragù on the base before adding the first layer of pasta.
4.
Layer ragù, béchamel, lasagne sheets, and grated Parmigiano-Reggiano, repeating until the dish is full. Finish with a generous layer of béchamel on top, dusted with Parmigiano. A good lasagne should have at least 4–5 layers for the best texture and flavour.
5.
Bake uncovered for 35–40 minutes until the top is golden and bubbling. Remove from the oven and rest for 15 minutes before cutting — this is crucial for clean slices. The lasagne will continue to set as it rests and will taste even better the next day.
