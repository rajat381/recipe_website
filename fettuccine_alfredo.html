Preparation time: 20 Min
Difficulty: HARD
Serves: 4-5 people
Category: Main Course
Posted by: Rajat
Calories 560
Protein 18g
Carbs 58g
Fat 28g
Saturates 17g
Fibre 2g
Sugar 3g
Salt 0.7g

Recipe Steps

1.
Cook fettuccine in a large pot of well-salted boiling water until al dente. The original Roman recipe uses only butter and Parmigiano-Reggiano — no cream. Reserve at least 1 cup of hot pasta cooking water before draining, as it is critical for emulsifying the sauce.
2.
While the pasta cooks, cut 100g of high-quality unsalted butter into small cubes and let it come to room temperature. Finely grate 150g of aged Parmigiano-Reggiano. The quality of these two ingredients is everything — do not substitute with pre-grated cheese.
3.
Drain fettuccine and immediately transfer to a warm serving bowl or back into the pot off the heat. Add the butter cubes and toss until melted. Add the grated Parmigiano a handful at a time, tossing constantly and adding a splash of hot pasta water between additions to create the sauce.
4.
Continue tossing and adding small amounts of pasta water until you have a glossy, creamy sauce that clings to every strand of fettuccine. The emulsion forms from the starch, fat, and heat working together — add pasta water gradually so the sauce does not become too thin.
5.
Season with freshly cracked black pepper and serve instantly in warmed bowls. Finish with an extra dusting of Parmigiano-Reggiano. Fettuccine Alfredo must be eaten immediately while the sauce is silky — it thickens and clumps quickly as it cools.
