Preparation time: 30 Min
Difficulty: EASY
Serves: 4-5 people
Category: Main Course
Posted by: Rajat
Calories 340
Protein 14g
Carbs 65g
Fat 6g
Saturates 1g
Fibre 8g
Sugar 4g
Salt 0.4g

Cooking Instructions

1.
Cook whole wheat pasta in generously salted boiling water for 1–2 minutes longer than the package suggests — whole wheat pasta needs extra time to become truly al dente without any raw, gritty texture. Reserve 1 cup of cooking water before draining.
2.
Heat 3 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil in a wide pan over medium heat. Add 3 sliced garlic cloves and cook until fragrant. Add halved cherry tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes until they begin to burst and release their juices into the oil.
3.
Add a large handful of baby spinach or kale to the pan and toss until just wilted, about 1–2 minutes. Season with salt, black pepper, and a pinch of chilli flakes if you like. The vegetables should be bright and lightly cooked, not mushy.
4.
Drain the whole wheat pasta and add it to the pan with the vegetables. Toss everything together over medium heat, adding splashes of reserved pasta water to help the sauce coat the pasta evenly. The earthy, nutty flavour of whole wheat pasta pairs beautifully with the tomatoes.
5.
Remove from heat. Squeeze over a little fresh lemon juice and scatter with toasted pine nuts or walnuts for crunch. Finish with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of good olive oil. Serve immediately as a light, nutritious, and satisfying meal.
